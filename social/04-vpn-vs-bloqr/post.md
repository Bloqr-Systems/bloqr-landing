# Your VPN Probably Isn't Solving the Problem You Think It Is

Most people buy a VPN for one of these reasons:
- Block ads and trackers
- Stop their ISP from seeing their activity
- Avoid malware
- "Just be more private online"

Here's what consumer VPNs actually do for those use cases:

**Ads and trackers?**
A VPN routes your traffic through a different server. Trackers follow sessions and fingerprints, not IP addresses. Most follow you anyway.

**ISP visibility?**
You traded your ISP's visibility for your VPN provider's visibility. You can't audit their logs. Their jurisdiction was chosen for legal reasons, not your protection.

**Malware?**
A VPN doesn't block malicious domains. It tunnels through them.

**"Just more private"?**
For the use case most VPN buyers actually have — yes, there's a better tool. DNS-level filtering blocks the trackers, ads, and malware domains before your device connects.
Faster. Cheaper. No new trust problem. Works on every device simultaneously.
