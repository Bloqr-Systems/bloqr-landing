# 🧹 What Is Browsing Hygiene?

You wash your hands before eating.
You don't drink water straight from a river.

Browsing hygiene is the same idea — applied to your internet connection.

**Here's the part most people don't know:**

Every time your device looks up a website, it sends a tiny request that says:
*"Where does this domain live?"*

Those requests are called DNS lookups, and by default:
- They travel unencrypted
- Your ISP can see every single one — even when you're on HTTPS
- Ad networks, trackers, and malware distributors operate on domains that respond to those lookups

Browsing hygiene means blocking the bad domains *before* your device ever connects to them.
No request = no tracker. No connection = no malware.

**The problem:** setting this up has always required technical expertise. Custom lists. Self-hosted servers. Manual updates.
